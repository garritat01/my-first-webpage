Merlin, Inc. Struggles to Bring Obsolete Technology to Market

Merlin Incorporated (MRLN) is an industry leader in retrofitting existing plane models with autonomous systems faces challenges in bringing its products to market. Merlin, Inc. explicitly designs its autonomous flight system —the Merlin Pilot—to retrofit existing aircraft models, not just new ones. Their technology is described as “aircraft-agnostic” and “designed for adaptation to any plane in operation today.”

Technology Shift

The war in Ukraine has led to great advancements in drone and autonomous flight. Industries and militaries around the world are rushing to process all the new advancements, leaving companies like Merlin, Inc. flat-footed. "This technology is like updating VCRs to play DVDs. The technology behind current autonomous add-ons is obsolete." Other companies such as Joby and Archer design their aircraft around autonomous flight systems, rather than try to repurpose old technology.

Financial Breakdown

Merlin is an early-stage aerospace/defense autonomy company with:

Very high net losses.
Minimal revenue.
High cash burn.
A recent influx of capital from a SPAC merger.
An auditor-flagged “going concern” warning (serious financial risk)

This combination means Merlin is not financially stable, but it has new capital to continue operations in the near term.

Is Merlin, Inc. a short-sell candidate?

Merlin has the financial profile of a company short sellers often target — but it also has the volatility and contract‑driven upside that can make shorting extremely risky. Merlin, Inc. is forge a path in an industry that is already overloaded with competition using obsolete technology.
